How it works

Three levers, one schedule.

Bright light. Low-dose melatonin. A stepwise sleep window. Tuned to the timezone you are flying into.

Light

Bright light at the right hour

Morning light pulls your clock earlier. Evening light pushes it later. The dominant effect — handled first by the schedule.

Melatonin

Low-dose melatonin, optional

0.3–0.5 mg timed to reinforce the shift, only if your country and your doctor allow it. Country-aware notes in Settings.

Sleep

Stepwise sleep window

Bedtime moves up to 1.5 hours per protocol day. Compresses what would be five days of fog into a couple.

Round trips

Three strategies. Picked for you.

Add a return leg and the planner picks the right shift mode based on how long you stay at the destination.

Stay-anchored · <3 days

Don't shift

For a flying visit, your body stays on home time. Round-trip shifts cost more than they pay back. We just protect your sleep.

Partial · 3–6 days

Halfway there

Body moves toward local time, not all the way. Coming home is gentle. The cap is computed from your time at destination.

Full · 7+ days

Full shift, both ways

Full shift to destination over the first protocol days, full shift back when you return. Standard long-haul mode.
